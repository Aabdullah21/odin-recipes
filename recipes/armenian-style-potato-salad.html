Armenian Style Potato Salad

Description

this Armenian-style potato salad is completely different. And completely delicious. Serve drizzled with more olive oil and garnished with more fresh mint.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled, quartered, and sliced into 1/4-inch pieces
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt, or more to taste
  • 1 tablespoon dried mint, divided
  • 1 large red onion, diced
  • ½ cup diced red bell pepper
  • ⅓ cup lemon juice, or more to taste
  • ½ cup thinly sliced green onions
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh mint
  • 2 teaspoons crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Steps

  • Transfer sliced potatoes into a large pot with enough cold water to cover the potatoes by a couple of inches. Stir in 1 rounded tablespoon kosher salt and 2 ½ teaspoons dried mint. Bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce heat to medium and let cook until tender, about 15 minutes, checking every 5 to 6 minutes for doneness. Drain well.
  • Transfer cooked potatoes to a large mixing bowl and let cool slightly.
  • While potatoes cool, heat ¼ cup olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add red onion, red bell pepper, garlic, and a pinch of salt; cook and stir for just 1 minute. Turn off the heat and stir in remaining dried mint.
  • Drizzle freshly squeezed lemon juice into potatoes and toss gently with a spatula. Add green onions, red onion mixture, remaining olive oil, fresh mint, fresh parsley, salt to taste, black pepper, chili flakes, and cayenne. Mix with a spatula until combined.
  • Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until chilled and flavors have melded, 3 to 4 hours.
  • Unwrap; stir, taste, and adjust seasonings as needed to personal tastes. The salad almost always needs more salt stirred in once it's tasted after being chilled. Serve.
